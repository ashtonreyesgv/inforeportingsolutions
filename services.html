What we do

Services that simplify complex reporting

Solutions to simplify complex tax information reporting and withholding requirements, minimize errors, and reduce penalties — from the first determination to the final return.

01

Advisory

Get the determination right before anything is filed. We help you classify payees and payments, apply the correct withholding, and document the reasoning behind it.

  • Guidance on Withholding Certificates (Forms W-8 and W-9)
  • Character of Payment Determinations and Sourcing Analysis
  • Withholding and Deposit Guidance
02

Operational Development

Turn one-off answers into a repeatable process your team can run. We build the procedures, train the people, and develop the tools that keep reporting on track year over year.

  • Process and Procedures
  • Training
  • Tool Development
03

Reporting Compliance

When it is time to file, we prepare and submit electronic returns accurately and on schedule — so the work you put into getting it right carries all the way through.

  • Reporting of Electronic Returns — Form 1099, Form 1042, and Form 1042-S
04

IRS Support

If a notice arrives or exposure surfaces, you do not have to face it alone. We respond to the IRS on the facts and guide voluntary disclosure where it is the right move.

  • Notice Response
  • Voluntary Disclosure
How we work

A clear path from question to compliance

Every engagement is tailored, but the shape is consistent — so you always know what happens next.

Step 01

Assess

We learn your payees, payments and processes, and pinpoint where the reporting risk actually sits.

Step 02

Determine

We resolve the hard questions — which form, which rate, which treaty — and document the reasoning.

Step 03

Implement

We file, or set your team up to file, with procedures and tools built around how you operate.

Step 04

Support

We stay available for notices, questions and the next reporting cycle as the rules evolve.

Let’s scope the right engagement

Tell us where you are — a single determination, a full reporting season, or an open IRS notice — and we’ll recommend the most efficient way forward.
